What elements or patterns seem universal?

The pattern of emphasizing filters and categories nearby search bar so that the user understands they have other methods of looking for the item without knowing the specific name for it.

The color scheme that directly represents the company’s brand identity. Certain colors such as red were used by some websites to highlight activities such as purchasing.

The focus of image representation of the product once user selects it. Most of the time selection of the certain image from the grid pattern leads to enlarged iaged and description of the item and price. It was also common for these pages to have a right-hand panel that allowed the user to customize the item that they selected.

The stand out pattern was definitely how Walmart integrated the purchase and quantity selection option. Most of the time user may oversee the option to increase the quantity of the item they’re purchasing, but because the purchase button automatically leads to suggesting different quantity, it is more likely to convince users to consume more than they thought they would before. I see big brands like Target or even Etsy employing this technique since they would want to convince their users to buy more of the same products they’re offering.
